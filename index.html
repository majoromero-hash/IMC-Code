Shifting the language is the first step toward a true partnership.

Proposed Language: Patient as Partner

Programs co-designed with patients

Tools developed with patient input

Including patients in defining needs

Actionable Recommendations for the IMC Code

1.

Introduce a "Patient as Partner" Principle

Formally recognize patients as essential collaborators in the design, implementation, and evaluation of all health initiatives.

2.

Mandate Patient Engagement

Require that Patient Support Programs and educational materials be developed with meaningful input from patients or their organizations.

3.

Augment the Code's Language

Systematically amend passive phrases ("for patients") to active, collaborative ones ("with patients").

Why This Matters

🎯

Better Health Outcomes

Partnership leads to programs that better meet patient needs, improving adherence and overall health.

🤝

Increased Trust

Transparency and collaboration build essential trust between the patient community and the industry.

💡

Meaningful Innovation

Patient insights are a critical driver for creating treatments and support systems that have a real-world impact.

It's time to amend the IMC Code. Let's make patients true partners in their health.
